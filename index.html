Swadeshi Movement started by Mahatma Gandhi

In India, Khadi is not just a cloth, it is a whole movement started by Mahatma Gandhi. The Khadi movement promoted an ideology, an idea that Indians could be self-reliant on cotton The khadi movement aimed at boycotting foreign goods including cotton and promoting Indian goods, thereby improving India's economy. Mahatma Gandhi began promoting the spinning of khadi in 1920s India thus making khadi an integral part and icon of the Swadeshi movement.

Know about khaadi!

Khadi or Khaddar is a term for handspun and hand-woven cloth from India, Bangladesh and Pakistan primarily made out of cotton. The cloth is primarily woven from cotton and may also include silk, or wool, which are all spun into yarn on a spinning wheel called a charkha. It is a versatile fabric, cool in summer and warm in winter. In order to improve the look, khadi/khaddar is sometimes starched to give it a stiffer feel. It is widely accepted in fashion circles.

Advantages of khaadi
